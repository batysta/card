Privacy Policy for SkyGallery

Last updated: April 8, 2026

SkyGallery is a gallery and backup app that helps you back up photos and videos to Telegram using your own Telegram account.

This Privacy Policy explains what data SkyGallery accesses, how it is used, and what choices you have.

1. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to the SkyGallery mobile application published by NovaApps Studio.

2. What SkyGallery does

SkyGallery:

  • reads photos and videos from your device, if you grant permission
  • connects to your Telegram account through Telegram user authentication
  • uploads selected photos and videos to a Telegram chat that you choose
  • loads your available Telegram chats so you can choose the cloud destination inside the app
  • reads media already stored in that selected Telegram chat
  • lets you sync, restore, download, share, and remove cloud media inside the app

In the current release, connecting Telegram during onboarding is required before the main tabs become available.

SkyGallery does not require you to create a separate account inside the app.

3. Information SkyGallery accesses

Depending on how you use the app, SkyGallery may access:

  • Photos and videos stored on your device
  • Media metadata such as file name, size, date, duration, album or folder name, width, and height
  • Optional photo metadata such as camera details and technical EXIF information when you enable those caption settings
  • Your Telegram phone number during sign-in
  • Telegram session data needed to keep your Telegram connection working on your device
  • The Telegram chats you load and the chat you select as your cloud destination
  • App preferences such as theme, language, upload quality settings, and metadata-sharing choices
  • Local backup and sync state, such as what was uploaded, what failed, and what is queued
  • Firebase measurement and stability data, which may include the app-instance ID, Android Advertising ID where available, approximate location derived from a masked IP address, app lifecycle data such as screen views and sessions, and technical crash or session metadata such as app version, Android version, SDK version, network type, device manufacturer, and model

4. How your information is used

SkyGallery uses this information only to provide app features such as:

  • showing your local gallery
  • matching local media with media already stored in Telegram
  • uploading media to Telegram
  • optionally attaching metadata to Telegram upload captions based on your settings
  • syncing the selected Telegram cloud chat
  • restoring, downloading, sharing, or removing remote media
  • showing queue, backup, and sync progress
  • storing app preferences and local status
  • measuring product usage, session quality, and app stability through Firebase Analytics, Firebase Sessions, and Firebase Crashlytics

5. Telegram and third-party services

SkyGallery uses Telegram through TDLib and your own Telegram account.

SkyGallery also uses Google Firebase services for product measurement and app stability, specifically Firebase Analytics, Firebase Sessions, and Firebase Crashlytics. Data handling on those services is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

SkyGallery is an independent third-party client. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Telegram FZ-LLC. Data handling on their servers is governed by the Telegram Privacy Policy.

When you choose to upload media, that media is sent to Telegram and becomes subject to Telegram's own terms, privacy practices, storage rules, and account limits.

In the normal product flow, sign-in data, sync data, and media travel between your device and Telegram's original servers. SkyGallery does not proxy that traffic through a NovaApps Studio backend to store your media.

If you keep metadata caption options enabled, camera details and technical EXIF fields available in a photo may also be included in the Telegram caption for that upload. The app does not offer GPS location sharing in captions.

If you choose a shared Telegram group or chat as your cloud destination, other people who already have access to that same Telegram space may also be able to see the same media there through Telegram or through their own SkyGallery setup.

SkyGallery does not operate its own cloud storage service for your media.

6. Does SkyGallery collect data on its own servers?

No — SkyGallery does not run its own backend for your media or Telegram account data, but the app does send limited telemetry to Google Firebase for analytics, session quality, and crash diagnostics.

SkyGallery is designed so that:

  • your gallery data is read locally on your device
  • your app preferences and sync state are stored locally on your device
  • your chosen cloud destination is Telegram, using your own Telegram account

SkyGallery does not send your photos, videos, or account data to NovaApps Studio servers.

Firebase Analytics, Sessions, and Crashlytics

The app includes Firebase Analytics, Firebase Sessions, and Firebase Crashlytics (Google). Based on Google's documentation for these Android SDKs, they may automatically collect:

  • app-instance ID
  • Android Advertising ID, where available on the device
  • coarse location derived from a masked IP address
  • app lifecycle and interaction data such as screen views and sessions
  • crash logs and technical metadata such as exception type, stack trace, app version, Android version, SDK version, network connection type, device manufacturer, and model
  • session quality metrics such as when the app is foregrounded to start a new session

In the current app code, SkyGallery does not set a custom Firebase Analytics user ID and does not log custom Firebase Analytics events that include your Telegram phone number, Telegram chat identifiers, or media file contents.

This Firebase data is used to understand product usage, monitor app quality, diagnose issues, and improve stability. Crash and diagnostic data is retained in accordance with the Firebase / Google settings configured for the project, and Google processes this data under Google's Privacy Policy.

7. Local storage on your device

SkyGallery stores some information locally on your device to function correctly, including:

  • app settings
  • selected backup folders
  • local upload and sync indexes
  • cache files and previews
  • temporary files used for upload preparation, previews, sharing, or video processing
  • encrypted local storage for sensitive Telegram configuration data where applicable

This local data helps the app avoid duplicate uploads, track backup status, and load media more efficiently.

8. Permissions

SkyGallery may request permissions such as:

  • photo and video access, to read your local media library
  • notification permission, to show backup and sync progress
  • foreground service/data sync permissions, so long-running uploads can continue more reliably

If you deny media permissions, some gallery, backup, and restore features may not work.

SkyGallery does not request GPS location permission and does not use ACCESS_MEDIA_LOCATION. If approximate location appears in Google Firebase analytics, it comes from masked IP processing by Google rather than device GPS.

9. Sharing and restore features

If you use sharing or restore features:

  • shared files may be exposed to another app only when you choose to share them
  • restored media is written back to your device storage through Android media APIs

SkyGallery does not silently share your media with other apps.

10. Data retention and deletion

You can stop using the app at any time.

You can also:

  • use the in-app "Disconnect and clear local data" action in SkyGallery Telegram settings
  • clear app data from Android settings
  • uninstall the app
  • remove media from your selected Telegram cloud chat
  • sign out and reconnect a different Telegram account

When you use "Disconnect and clear local data", SkyGallery logs out the current Telegram session on this device and removes local settings, backup selections, sync indexes, entries from the local telephotos_next.db database, cached previews and cloud cache files, and the local TDLib session directory. This process does not delete media already stored in Telegram.

Clearing app data from Android settings also removes locally stored settings, sync indexes, and cached files on the device, but does not delete media already stored in Telegram unless you delete it from Telegram.

11. Children

SkyGallery is not specifically directed to children.

We do not knowingly collect data from children under the age of 13.
